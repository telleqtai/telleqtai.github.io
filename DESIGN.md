# Telleqt Website Design Requirements

## 1. Company Name

**Telleqt**

Telleqt is an AI technology company. The website should present Telleqt as a serious, modern, enterprise-grade AI transformation and technology partner for large companies, mid-sized organizations, investors, and strategic partners.

The website must create a strong positive impression and communicate that Telleqt is capable of helping organizations solve complex AI transformation challenges in real operational environments.

The overall impression should be:

- Professional
- Modern
- Enterprise-grade
- Trustworthy
- Technically strong
- Calm and confident
- Focused on measurable business value
- Attractive to investors and strategic partners

---

## 2. Core Website Message

The most important message must appear on the first screen of the website.

### Main Hero Headline

**AI Transformation for Companies That Have Already Tried and Failed**

### Hero Subheadline

**Telleqt helps organizations restart stalled AI initiatives with a practical, personalized, implementation-first approach focused on measurable cost savings and operational impact.**

### Core Meaning

Many organizations have already tried AI transformation. They hired consultants, launched pilots, created strategy documents, tested tools, and spent budgets — but still failed to reach real business impact.

Telleqt is built for these companies.

Telleqt does not start with months of abstract analysis or heavy consulting documentation. Instead, Telleqt combines diagnosis, prioritization, and early implementation in parallel, so clients can see first practical AI use cases working within the first months.

The website should immediately make the visitor feel:

> “This is for us. We already tried AI transformation, it did not work, and Telleqt understands our situation.”

---

## 3. Main Positioning

Telleqt helps organizations restart AI transformation after failed attempts.

The positioning should emphasize that Telleqt is not just another AI vendor, AI agency, or consulting company. Telleqt combines technology, implementation, business understanding, and practical use case delivery.

### Key Messaging Themes

#### Failed AI Transformation Restart

Telleqt helps companies that have already attempted AI transformation several times but failed to achieve meaningful results.

#### Practical Implementation

Telleqt does not spend months preparing theoretical reports before action. Diagnosis, prioritization, and implementation happen in parallel.

#### Personalized Approach

Every company has its own systems, data, people, constraints, failed attempts, and operational reality. Telleqt designs AI transformation around the client’s actual environment.

#### Cost Savings and Optimization

Telleqt focuses on AI use cases that create measurable value, especially cost savings, operational efficiency, automation, process optimization, and better decision-making.

#### Measurable Business Impact

The goal is not AI for the sake of AI. The goal is business impact that can be seen, tested, measured, and scaled.

---

Recommended pages:

# 6. Approach Page Requirements

## Page Title

**A Practical Approach to AI Transformation**

## Page Subtitle

Telleqt combines diagnosis, prioritization, and early implementation to help organizations move from AI ambition to measurable operational impact.

## Page Goal

This page should explain how Telleqt works and why the approach is different from traditional consulting-heavy AI transformation.

---

## 6.1 Approach Steps

### 1. Diagnose

We understand the company’s business goals, data landscape, systems, bottlenecks, previous AI attempts, and operational constraints.

### 2. Prioritize

We identify the AI use cases with the highest potential for cost savings, automation, efficiency, and measurable business value.

### 3. Implement Early

We do not wait for a long strategy phase to finish. We begin building first practical use cases in parallel with diagnosis and prioritization.

### 4. Prove Value

We focus on working solutions that can be demonstrated, measured, and improved in real operational conditions.

### 5. Scale

Successful use cases can then be expanded into production systems, integrated with business processes, data flows, and operational teams.

---

## 6.2 Suggested Visual

A horizontal or vertical process diagram:

**Diagnose → Prioritize → Implement Early → Prove Value → Scale**

The diagram should look clean and enterprise-grade. It should not feel like a generic consulting process slide. It should feel more like a practical execution model.
